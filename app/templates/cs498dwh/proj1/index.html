Part I: Hybrid Images

Best Hybrid

The following hybrid between White house and Taj mahal turned out pretty well, from close you see the White house, and from a distance you'll see Taj mahal. (Click for larger versions) The White House image is filtered with a laplacian filter, which is simply applying the gaussian filter and then subtracting the resultant image from original, which gives a sharp image as shown. Taj Mahal is filtered using a gaussian filter, blurring it. I tried a lot of different sigmas until I finally had a good result. the trick is to have wide apart cut-off frequencies for the two gaussian filters. If the cut-off frequencies overlap, an unsatisfactory image results as depicted in the MIT paper with the angry man and strict woman.

Part II: Image Enhancement

Contrast Enhancement: I tried Gamma correction and Histogram equalization, for 2 images (have shown one here) and both times Gamma correction seemed to give a better result. The clouds seem to have been pixelized due to histogram equalization

 Gamma Corrected Strong Histogram equalized Averaged with original picture
Color Enhancement: To make colors brighter and the image more vivid, we convert image to hsv color space, and then increase the saturation, however care must be taken to keep the saturation value between 0 and 1, so we raise it to a power between 0 and 1.


Color Shift: I converted the image to LAB color space, and then to make the image more red I increased 'a' by a certain constant and to make it less yellow (and therefore more blue) I decreased 'b' by a certain constant (after a bit of experimentation). Then I converted the image back to rgb space.
